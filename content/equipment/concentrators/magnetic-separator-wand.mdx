## The Material Nobody Talks About

When you clean out your sluice box or high banker after a day in the field, you're not just collecting gold — you're collecting everything the riffles trapped. Magnetite is high on that list. This dense black iron oxide mineral is almost everywhere gold is found, and it's heavy enough to settle right alongside your gold in every riffle system you run. By the time your concentrates are bagged up for the drive home, black sand and magnetite might make up 95% or more of your total concentrate volume.

Here's why that matters: every cleanup tool you own — Blue Bowl, spiral wheel, Gold Cube, hand pan — has to work through all of that black sand to find the gold. The more magnetic black sand you can remove before you start, the faster and cleaner your cleanup runs. You're reducing noise so the signal is easier to find.

The magnetic separator wand removes the magnetic fraction in minutes. For $12. It's the least dramatic tool in your kit and the one you'll reach for first every single cleanup session.

## What You're Actually Buying

The wand is two components: a rare earth (neodymium) magnet and a plastic or stainless steel sleeve. The magnet lives inside the sleeve. You drag the wand through your concentrates — wet or dry — and the magnet pulls magnetite and any magnetic minerals through the sleeve wall, coating the outside. When you want to release the captured material, you slide the sleeve off the magnet over a separate container. The material drops cleanly away from the magnet into the discard container, and you repeat.

That sleeve design is the entire reason you buy a purpose-built wand instead of just taping a magnet to a chopstick. Without the sleeve, releasing captured material is a mess — you're trying to scrape magnetic black sand off a bare magnet with your fingers, flinging material everywhere. With the sleeve, release is a one-second motion and your captured fraction goes exactly where you want it.

The magnet grade matters. Look for N52 neodymium — it's the strongest commercially available grade and dramatically speeds up the separation process. Weaker magnets work but require more passes and more time. A strong N52 wand pulled through a cup of concentrates once typically removes 60–80% of the magnetic fraction. A second pass gets you to 90%+. That's all you need before running the remaining material through your concentrator.

## Using the Wand in Practice

The most common workflow: let your concentrates dry completely (or at least partially), spread them in a flat shallow pan or on a sheet of dark paper, and drag the wand slowly through the material in parallel passes. You'll see the magnetite and magnetic black sand leap up to the sleeve. Slide the sleeve off over a separate container, dump the captured material, replace the sleeve, and repeat until the passes are coming up clean.

You can also run the wand through wet concentrates. It's slightly less efficient — the magnetic material has more resistance in water — but it works if you want to do a quick magnetic pull before running the Blue Bowl rather than waiting for your concentrates to dry. Either approach is valid; dry works better if time allows.

After two or three wand passes, the concentrate volume is typically 30–70% smaller depending on how magnetic your area's black sand is. What's left is still not clean gold — you have non-magnetic black sand (hematite, fine garnet, cassiterite, and other dense non-magnetic minerals) still in the mix. But you've dramatically reduced the volume your Blue Bowl or Gold Cube has to process, which means faster runs, less re-processing, and better recovery on the final fine gold.

## The Impact on Everything Downstream

This is the part that doesn't get emphasized enough. The magnetic wand doesn't just save you time on its own — it actively improves the performance of every other cleanup tool you use.

The Blue Bowl's water vortex works best when the material you're feeding is relatively homogeneous in density. A heavy load of magnetite muddies the water action and requires multiple runs to fully clean. Remove the magnetite first and the Blue Bowl separates the remaining material more cleanly in a single pass.

The Gold Cube's vortex mats capture heavy material across three stages — but flooding those mats with magnetite means they're holding a lot of material that isn't gold. A pre-wand pass reduces the load on the mats and lets them focus on the gold-bearing fraction.

Even hand-panning is faster and more accurate after a wand pass. You're not spending the first ten minutes of your pan washing off a thick layer of magnetite — you're working a cleaner, denser concentrate from the start.

Every downstream step in your cleanup chain runs better when the upstream magnetic pull is done right. A $12 wand making a $400 Gold Cube work measurably better is the definition of high-leverage gear.

## What It Doesn't Do

Be clear on what the wand doesn't remove: non-magnetic black sand. Hematite, ilmenite (partially magnetic but not strongly), fine garnet, cassiterite, zircon, and other dense heavy minerals won't stick to the magnet. After a thorough wand pass, your concentrates still contain non-magnetic fines that need to be separated from gold through gravity methods — Blue Bowl, spiral wheel, hand pan.

The wand is a first-stage prep tool, not a finishing tool. Its job is to reduce your concentrate volume and simplify the work for whatever comes next. It does that job extremely well. Just don't expect a wand pass to give you clean gold — it's one step in a multi-step process, and a very important first step.

## Which Wand to Buy

A search on Amazon for "magnetic separator wand gold mining" returns a range of options from $6 to $30. The differences are mostly in magnet strength and build quality. Here's what to look for:

**Magnet grade:** N52 neodymium is the target. If the listing doesn't specify grade, assume it's weaker and consider a different option. N52 is noticeably faster to work with.

**Sleeve material:** Stainless steel sleeves are more durable and clean up more easily than plastic. If you're deciding between two otherwise-equal options, go stainless.

**Length:** 6 to 10 inches is the practical range. Longer wands let you sweep a wider area per pass; shorter wands give you more control in a small pan. 8 inches is a good middle ground.

At $8–$20, the price range is close enough that there's no reason to buy the cheapest version. Spend the middle of the range on a well-reviewed N52 wand with a stainless sleeve and you'll use it for years. Since ASINs change frequently in this product category and multiple vendors sell equivalent wands, search Amazon with those specs in mind rather than buying based on any specific listing.

## The Kit That Makes Sense

Every cleanup kit needs a magnetic separator wand. Full stop. It doesn't matter whether you're running a Blue Bowl, a Gold Cube, a spiral wheel, or just a hand pan — the wand goes in the kit before any of those purchases. The cost-to-impact ratio is unmatched anywhere in gold mining equipment.

If you're building a cleanup kit from scratch, buy the wand first. Then the Blue Bowl. Everything else comes after you've established you need more than one stage of separation.

If you've been running concentrates through your cleanup tools without pre-pulling the magnetic fraction first, try it once. One wand pass before your next Blue Bowl session will change how you work concentrates permanently.

## Compared to the Alternatives

**Strong neodymium fridge magnet in a sandwich bag (~$0):** It works. The magnet pulls the magnetite just fine. The problem is releasing the material — you open the bag over your concentrates to strip it off, and material goes everywhere. The sliding sleeve design is the functional upgrade that justifies the $12. Spend the money.

**Electric rare earth roll separator (~$600):** A completely different product category — a continuously-fed electric conveyor with controlled rare earth magnets for high-volume industrial-grade separation. If you're processing volumes large enough to consider this, you already know you need it. For hobby miners, the handheld wand is the right tool.
